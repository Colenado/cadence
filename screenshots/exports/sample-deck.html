01 — The problemJun 30, 2026
01

The problem

Across Q1 and Q2, average ramp time for new field sales reps in the Western region hit 91 days, against a target of 60. Eight of the last twelve hires missed their first-quarter quota entirely. Exit interviews and skip-level conversations point to a consistent gap: reps don't know how to position the new product line, can't navigate the demo environment confidently, and lack a clear playbook for the first 30 days in territory.

Affected12–18 per cohort, 4 cohorts / year × new field sales hires
Current state91 days
Cost$1.4M annually — $890K productivity loss, $510K turnover cost
This is the business case the budget conversation lives inside.

02 — Root causeJun 30, 2026
02

Root cause

Performance data and conversation analysis from 18 new hire cohorts show three consistent blockers: a 4-week gap between start date and first product training, inconsistent peer shadowing, and no structured playbook for the first 30 days in territory.

Evidence: Reps who had a peer mentor hit quota 2.3x more often than those who didn't. We've ruled out compensation, hiring profile, and manager coaching quality. This isn't a will problem, it's a system problem.

Ruled out: Compensation, hiring profile, manager coaching quality

Make clear: this is a system problem, not a will problem.
